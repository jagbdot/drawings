Drawings

"Layer of spontaneity"

During the filming of this documentary, illustrator Victoria Leader joined us for the main production and documented the life of the salt workers in an artistic and tangible way, adding an extra component to the entire project. These drawings were mostly created on the spot, creating a layer of spontaneity and tangibleness to moments that will never repeat itself.

[1]. Mrs. Laurita - Different Drawings.

The hand-drawings of Victoria Leader are raw and honest, such as the salt workers that we met during this project. These are memories that will live forever as a testament of who the salt workers of maras were in this specific period of time (2019).

[2]. Thomas Silcock and Toto Thots while filming for the documentary.

"Raw and honest"

Victoria sketches from life, in real time, as a way to visually document encounters and observations while moving through the world. Her work in Maras began as on-site drawings and paintings that reveal a little of the behind the scenes process, an additional perspective on the relationship between filmmaker and subject. The final images were then reproduced using a fine line paint brush and opaque watercolor.
